Chair selection protocol — 3-step process

Who becomes the ABC chair.
How. By whom.

A documented, transparent sequence endorsed by the HC. Every selection follows the same three steps. Every step has a status. No ambiguity about who decides.

The selection process takes 30 days from launch to endorsement. It produces a chair with full legitimacy among partners and formal sign-off from the HC/HCT.

Designed for contexts where leadership legitimacy is contested and operational independence of the chair must be demonstrable to donors and the broader humanitarian system.

30
Days from launch to HC endorsement
50%
Minimum quorum of operational partners required to vote
1
Mandatory HC/HCT endorsement required to activate mandate
01
Open call in zone

A formal call for candidatures is issued to all operational partners present in the zone. Any INGO or NNGO with 6+ months of active presence may apply.

HINGO Forum issues call — 5-day window
Candidate submits 1-page expression of interest
Eligibility criteria: presence, neutrality, language, availability
OCHA sub-office validates applications
Duration: 10 days
02
Partner vote

All operational partners present in the zone vote. Quorum: 50% must participate. Simple majority selects the chair. HINGO Forum facilitates — does not vote.

Written ballot — no show of hands
One vote per organisation regardless of size
Result communicated within 24 hours
Losing candidates notified with written rationale
Duration: 5 days
03
HC/HCT endorsement

The elected candidate is formally endorsed by the Humanitarian Coordinator. This gives the chair authority to convene inter-cluster meetings and represent the zone at HCT level.

HINGO submits result and candidate profile to HC
HC endorsement letter within 5 working days
Mandate begins day after endorsement
Duration: 12 months, renewable once by partner vote
Duration: 10–15 days
